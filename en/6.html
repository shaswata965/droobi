What is Diabetes?

Most of the food we eat is turned into glucose, or sugar, for our bodies to use for energy. The pancreas, an organ that lies near the stomach, makes a hormone called insulin that helps glucose get into the cells of our bodies. Insulin serves as a “key” to open your cells and to allow the glucose to enter so your body can use the glucose for energy. But with diabetes, this system does not work.

When you have diabetes, your body either doesn't make enough insulin or can't use its own insulin as well as it should. This causes sugar levels to rise in your blood, urine, saliva, sweats & all body excretions.

Uncontrolled Diabetes can cause serious health complications, including heart disease, blindness, kidney failure, and lower-extremity amputations.
