Type 1 Diabetes

In people with type 1 diabetes, little or no insulin is being produced by the pancreas. Insulin is a hormone needed to allow sugar (glucose) to enter cells for energy.

As a result, sugar builds up in the blood instead of being used as energy. Your body uses your fat for energy as you are not getting energy from glucose. This results in sudden weight loss, even if you are constantly eating.

Type 1 diabetes can develop at any age, but it is commonly diagnosed in children and young adults below 30 years old. Coma and death can result from delayed diagnosis and treatment of Type 1 diabetes. Only about 10% of all people with diabetes have type 1 diabetes.

Currently, type 1 diabetes has no cure. A person with type 1 diabetes must be treated with insulin everyday. Insulin is absolutely required for patients with type 1 diabetes, Without insulin, glucose continues to build up in the bloodstream. Since the cells are not getting energy from glucose, body fat is used for energy. This accounts for the sudden weight loss, even though the person is constantly hungry and eating. Burning a lot of fat very quickly causes a buildup of acids called ketones. If ketones become excessive, it can lead to a life-threatening condition called diabetic ketoacidosis or DKA.

Therefore, treatment focuses on managing blood sugar levels with insulin, diet and lifestyle management to prevent complications.
