How does your food journey when you don't have diabetes?

Metabolism refers to the processes that occur in the body once food is eaten.

A person without diabetes maintains blood glucose in a “normal” range no matter what they eat.

  • Food consumed changes to glucose (sugar) during digestion. This causes your blood sugar levels to rise.

  • Glucose enters the bloodstream

  • Pancreas responds to the presence of food by releasing insulin

  • Insulin allows glucose from the blood to enter into your body’s cells where the glucose can be used for energy

  • This causes your blood glucose levels to drop back to normal levels.
