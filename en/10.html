Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes occurs when the body cannot use insulin normally (also known as insulin resistance), and or does not make enough insulin. Type 2 diabetes is a progressive condition. Eventually, the pancreas cannot keep up with the demand and insulin production starts to decline. As a result, sugar builds up in the blood instead of being used as energy.

Type 2 diabetes is characterized by the body being unable to metabolize glucose (a simple sugar). This leads to high levels of blood glucose, which over time may damage the organs of the body. This is why it is important for people with diabetes to eat healthily.

The good news is that for many people with type 2 diabetes, it can be controlled by eating healthy, performing physical activity and taking medication, thereby limiting their risk of developing long term complications.
