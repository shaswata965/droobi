How does your food journey when you have diabetes?

  • Glucose reaches the blood the same way as in people without diabetes.

  • Glucose stays in the blood, because it is not getting into the body cells. This could be due to three reasons:

    1. Not enough insulin (Little or No Insulin)
    2. Your body cells are not responding to insulin, a term called insulin resistance
    3. Delay in insulin release from your pancreas

  • Excess glucose may be released from the liver.

  • Glucose levels in the blood rise.

  • Kidneys begin to remove some of the excess glucose when levels are above 180mg/dl
