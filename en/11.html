Lesson 1
Gestational Diabetes

Some women develop gestational diabetes during pregnancy. This is because the hormones during pregnancy do not allow your cells to use insulin properly (insulin resistance). Women with obesity and family history of diabetes are at high risk for developing gestational.

If your body cannot supply enough insulin to overcome the resistance, your blood glucose levels will rise. High blood glucose levels during pregnancy can result in a very large baby as well as other birth complications.

After the baby is born, gestational diabetes normally goes away. However, if you have gestational diabetes, you have a high risk of developing type 2 diabetes. Up to half of the women with gestational diabetes will develop type 2 diabetes within the next 5 to 10 years. This risk can be lowered by maintaining a reasonable body weight and staying physically active.
